# Manual Review Cases

## Overview

While most customers can be assigned to a retention segment through automated business rules, certain customers require manual evaluation because their behavioural indicators provide conflicting signals.

The following fifteen customers were selected for manual review. These customers exhibit combinations of return behaviour, support activity, spending patterns and engagement metrics that make straightforward retention decisions difficult.

Each case below includes the current segment assignment, observed behaviour, recommended action and business justification.

---

# Customer 1 – CUST00438

## Current Segment
Regular Customers

## Observed Behaviour
- Recency: 124 days
- Frequency: 3 orders
- Monetary Value: ₹2466.39
- Support Tickets: 3
- Return Rate: 100%

## Why Review Is Required
The customer has relatively strong historical spending compared to other Regular Customers but has generated multiple support tickets and returned all recorded purchases.

## Recommended Action
Customer support outreach before promotional targeting.

## Business Justification
Addressing dissatisfaction is more important than offering discounts. Revenue recovery depends on solving the underlying customer issue.

# Conclusion

These fifteen customers were selected because their behaviour does not fit cleanly into a single retention category. Automated segmentation provides an initial recommendation, but human review is necessary to determine the most appropriate retention strategy. Focusing on these edge cases improves decision quality and reduces the risk of both unnecessary retention spending and preventable customer churn.
